## About the Central Counties Golf Association

ALCC's CCGA Delegates are **Darold Boone** (cwbfan77@hotmail.com) and
**Tom Newingham** (tnewy@yahoo.com). The Central
Counties Golf Association is a long-running regional series bringing
together golfers from 14 member clubs across the region: American Legion
Country Club, Belles Springs, Clearfield-Curwensville, Down River,
Huntingdon Country Club, Iron Masters, Lewistown Country Club, Lost Creek,
Mountain View, Nittany Country Club, Park Hills, Philipsburg Country Club,
Sinking Valley, and Summit Country Club.

## Paul Bradley Central Counties Junior Scholarship

The CCGA offers the Paul Bradley Junior Scholarship to member-club juniors
in grades 9–12 who have played in at least two CCGA events during the
season. Applications are due June 1 of a student's senior year — contact
the pro shop or CCGA delegate for the current application.

## A Century of Central Counties Golf

Newspaper coverage of the Central Counties Golf Association traces back
to at least 1927, when 32 golfers from 9 clubs competed in a qualifying
tournament at the Blairmont Country Club course. By 1941, the association
had grown to 11 member clubs for its 18th annual tournament, hosted at
Tyrone.

ALCC's own golfers have a long record in the event: Elinor Wible won the
Women's Golf Association crown in 1953, beating the defending champion
1-up on the 20th hole. Ed Strickler (Huntingdon) won his 8th Central
Counties title in 1985 with a comeback over the final round. In 1994,
Greg Ferguson (SVCC) won by 2 strokes over 36 holes at Penn State's Blue
Course, with 73 golfers representing 8 of the association's then-13
member clubs.
