---
title: "The ALCC Pool"
summary: "The club's swimming pool, part of ALCC since 1928, closed in recent years due to a significant leak."
order: 8
---

Established in **1928**, alongside the clubhouse, the pool at ALCC was long
a cornerstone of the club experience. For generations it provided a place
for families to gather, with a deep end and diving board for the
adventurous and a shallow section for younger swimmers. From **Memorial Day
to Labor Day**, countless members enjoyed relaxing summer days by the
water.

In recent years, however, the pool developed a significant leak, ultimately
leading to its closure. Safety concerns, combined with the escalating cost
of repairs, made it impractical to reopen without major investment. While
the American Legion Home Association has explored several fundraising
opportunities, the necessary funds have not yet been secured.

As a result, the pool area will be filled in at some point in the near
future. At this time, there are no specific plans for how that space will
be repurposed.

Though it's disappointing to close a chapter that has been such a
meaningful part of ALCC's history, the club remains committed to preserving
and strengthening its future through thoughtful improvements and long-term
planning.
